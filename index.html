About Me
Voyage into the unkown

Before I was a designer, I was set on becoming a biotech researcher developing new medical treatments and therapeutic drugs for our aging population. Then I was introduced to design and ended up graduating with a double major in design and psychology. As it turns out, the same thing that drew me to designing personal treatments also drew me to designing personal experiences — a drive for understanding underlying problems and positively affecting people on a broad scale.

During my time at Davis, I’ve worked with awesome people at places like The California Aggie, TACL-LYF, Design Careers Club, and Gale's group study. It’s been a great learning experience, and though my formal design education focused more on visual communications, I want to dive deeper into product design and better understand what makes people tick.
